A. The Importance of the Trinity

The doctrine of the trinity affects how we understand God and his purposes for creating us, His love, His unity, and most especially Jesus’ example of submission and obedience. In fact we can only truly under­stand what it is to be a disciple of the Lord if we under­stand the truth of the trinity. Without the truth of the trinity, the primary requirement of discipleship to deny oneself take up one’s cross and follow Jesus would not mean anything. The laying down of one’s own will to do the will of another is at the very core of true Biblical discipleship. What example could Jesus have left us if he were the Father? Would not in all truth he be doing his own will? Then why the struggle in the garden before his crucifixion (Lk 22:42) to not do his will but the will of his Father? If he was the Father and the Father’s will was his very own? If Jesus were already the Father, then there could be no struggle to do the Father’s will for it would have been his own already! Thus, the very struggle of Jesus in the garden to lay down his will and do the Father’s will (i.e. not my will be done but thine) can only be if the Father and Jesus are two distinct persons.
Thus, we see Jesus and the Father had their own separate wills. In fact everything that makes two people two people is what we see between the Father and Jesus. They not only had their own will they also had their own separate knowledge of what they knew: “But of that day and that hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels which are in heaven, neither the Son, but the Father.” Mark 13:32 Now let no one deceive you friend, Jesus was fully God while he was here on this earth and though he knew all things, yet he did not know what was in his Father’s heart as to when the Father would send him back for the second coming.

A. Two or Three Witnesses

Is the trinity taught in the Bible? Yes! “For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one. And there are three that bear witness in earth, the Spirit, and the water, and the blood: and these three agree in one.” 1 John 5:7-8 This could not be any simpler there are three that are one NOT as in the same or identical, but ONE in witness, one in agree­ment. That is the trinity. Sadly, Oneness people completely twist this verse to say the opposite of what it is saying. Clearly, from the context the Spirit, the water, and the blood are not one as in the same or identical, but again one in agreement.
This is required in order to bear reliable witness. We must have 2 or 3 witnesses! Forget not the principle of witness: “at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be estab­lished.” This principle is established many times in the scripture (Dt 19:15, 17:6, Mt 18:16, 1Tim 5:19, Heb 10:28). Thus, for a matter to be established there must be two or three independent witnesses. If God the Father and His Son Jesus are not two persons, neither can they be two witnesses. It is clearly seen from John 8:16-18 by Jesus’ own words that He and the Father are two separate persons. Together with the Holy Spirit the three are one.
A matter cannot be established by one giving their testimony, leaving the room putting on a different face or mask and then returning to give the same or corroborating testimony. NO! The eyewitnesses must be separate individuals whom you notice each have their own mouth. Thus, if Jesus is the Father, and Jesus is the Son, and Jesus is the Spirit we have only one witness, which even by Jesus’ own words this would make his witness invalid by his own laws.

In the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established.” This is not saying that the witness may be established by coming two or three times, for then we could establish anything by just saying it over & over. That establishes nothing. Rather, Paul had come on both his first and second journeys with others who also witnessed to what he was saying and so it would be on this third trip also.
Some Jesus onlyers have said that Jesus as the Son of God was only during his time here on earth, but this is clearly contrary to what the scripture says. Note also: “Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our profession.” (Heb 4:13) Jesus said concerning those who overcome, “I will confess his name before my Father, and before his angels.” (Rev 3:5)
